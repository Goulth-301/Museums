Hip-Hop Exhibition in Silesian Museum in Katowice

Visiting the exhibition, we can learn a lot of interesting facts about hip-hop music and culture. The exhibition was created in collaboration with the hip-hop community. The exhibition features items from private collections, such as photographs from the 90s, cassettes, VHS tapes, furniture, personal belongings, and clothing. We’ll be walking into the 1990s Silesian housing estates, with their clotheslines, playgrounds, courtyards, sports facilities, and private garages where songs were created..

When new meets the old

Castle Museum in Pszczyna

The documented origins of the stone defensive castle in Pszczyna date back to the first half of the 15th century, as it is known that in 1433 it withstood a Hussite invasion. The entire complex was protected by an earthen rampart and a moat. In 1517, Kazimierz II, Duke of Cieszyn, sold the estates to the Hungarian magnate Alex Turzo, which ended the Piast dynasty’s rule over the Pszczyna region. The Free State of Pszczyna (comprising Pszczyna, Mikołów, Bieruń, and, until 1536, Mysłowice) was established, and Pszczyna became its capital.

Saturn Museum of Czeladź

The Saturn Museum is a relatively new institution, established in 2009 on the basis of the Czeladź Chamber of Tradition, which had been in existence since 2003. Its primary mission is to collect artifacts and all tangible and intangible memorabilia related to the history of Czeladź, including the mining industry, which shaped the city’s character for decades, as well as to research, document, and promote the history of the city and the region. The museum is housed in two buildings. Since its inception, its headquarters have been the so-called Palace under the Pillars—once the villa of the director of the “Saturn” mine. In 2016, the museum expanded to include a second building—a beautifully restored power plant, also a relic of the mine’s former operations. The institution also administers the historic Jewish cemetery on Będzińska Street.
